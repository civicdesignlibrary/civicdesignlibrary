The Civic Design Library is a crowdsourced data set of civic design resources created by global practitioners working at the intersection of design and the public sector.

It aims to showcase the breadth and depth of civic design and offer data and visual formats for peer-to-peer learning and inspiration. We're getting started by prototyping how to source and display case studies that capture the details and impact of design projects in the public sector. The case studies provide examples of ways of working, deployed or speculative solutions, and areas of impact that may be reproduced by other organizations seeking to do similar work. They can be submitted by in-house or contracted designers, project sponsors, funders, or other project partners using the submission form. We will soon launch additional elements including resources such as articles, books, and podcasts, as well as sharing of design artifacts such as journey maps, research plans, and solutions.
